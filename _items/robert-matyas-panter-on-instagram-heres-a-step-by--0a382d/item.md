title: "Robert Matyas | PANTER on Instagram: 'Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to create 3D text with grain on shadows in Illustrator.

First select your text, right click on the text and select “Create outline”.

Next, go to Effects > 3D and Materials > 3D (Classic) > Extrude & Bevel (Classic). Here increase the extrusion if needed and adjust the position of the text or letter.

After that you can go to expand it by going to “Object > Expand appearance”.

Then right click on text and select “Ungroup”, ungroup it 2x so this way you can deconstruct the 3D extrusion.

Select every extrusion and go to “Pathfinder and click on Unite”. Do this with each.

Add gradients to the extrusion and recolor them.

Duplicate the extrusion by pressing Ctrl+C and then Ctrl+F, this way is going to paste another copy on top of the shape.

Recolor the top pasted extrusions gradient to black & white.

Then go to “Effects > Texture and select Grain”. Make sure to change the grain type to “Stipple” and adjust the settings to your liking.

Not but least go to Opacity and change the Blend mode to Multiply.

Repeat these steps with each extrusion by selecting all the extrusion and applying the grain at once.

You also can increase or decrease the grain by scaling up and down the letter or text.
